**_User Habits_**

(Understanding what users already do and how)

## ## Question 1 - Applications Used

**Do you currently use one or more apps to keep track of media (movies, series, books, games)? If yes, which ones?**

- **Oscar:** No.
- **Valérie:** No, I didn’t know such apps existed.
- **Théo:** No, I use Google News for video games.
- **Julien:** I use Letterboxd and YouTube playlists. (For books, I use Google Keep.)

## Question 2 - Main Use

**How do you usually use this type of app? (history, content discovery, reviews, recommendations, other…)**

- **Oscar:** No main use.
- **Valérie:** I usually use TikTok or YouTube to see reviews of things that might interest me.
- **Théo:** /
- **Julien:** I like seeing the history of what I’ve watched. It’s cool to see what others have watched, their watchlists, etc. I like getting recommendations for similar movies and ranking the ones I’ve already seen (only movies!).
I also use Steam for games and How Long to Beat to know how long it takes to finish a game.
For shows and anime, I’d love a similar app that tells you how many hours they take to finish.

## Question 3 - After Finishing a Work

**When you finish a work (movie, series, book, or game), what do you usually do? (talk about it, rate it, recommend it, forget it, other…)**

- **Oscar:** I talk about it with people to see if they’ve seen or played it.
- **Valérie:** I talk about it and recommend it to my friends in person.
- **Théo:** I use Discord to discuss with people who play similar games. For movies and books, I don’t.
- **Julien:** For games and movies, I talk with friends and rate them on Letterboxd. For books, I don’t do anything because I don’t have an app to organize them.

## Question 4 - Main Device

**On which device do you mostly use this type of app? (smartphone, tablet, computer, other)**

- **Oscar:** I don’t use any, but otherwise it would be PC and mobile.
- **Valérie:** —
- **Théo:** I use my phone for media, but I don’t have this kind of app.
- **Julien:** I mainly use the computer, but also quite a bit on my phone (mostly to talk about it with friends).

## Question 5 - Tracking Releases

**Do you pay attention to upcoming releases (movies, series, books, games)?**
**If yes, how do you currently follow them?**

- **Oscar:** Either I already know, or I hear through word of mouth or YouTube.
- **Valérie:** Yes, when I’m really interested in something, I research it online.
- **Théo:** Yes! Through social media, YouTube, Twitter, or Metacritic via Google.
- **Julien:** For movies, yes: I check recommendations from people or specialized Instagram accounts. Same for games. For books, I look at friends’ recommendations, Instagram, or the author’s other works.

## Question 6 - Ratings and Reviews

**Do you like rating or giving your opinion on works you consume? Why?**

- **Oscar:** Yes, especially to my friends.
- **Valérie:** Yes, because I like debating and sharing opinions with others who’ve seen it, to compare perspectives.
- **Théo:** Yes, to share my opinion, but not necessarily to rate. I like giving feedback and suggesting improvements, but I think ratings are pointless.
- **Julien:** Yes, I like debating scores when several people have seen the same thing. It’s also practical to sort by rating, both to give and get recommendations.

**_Frustrations and Difficulties_**

(Identifying problems, limits, and pain points)

## Question 7 - Pros and Cons

**What are the pros and cons of the apps you currently use?**
**Are there any useless or missing features?**

- **Oscar:** I give ratings when I really enjoyed the work, to support it.
- **Valérie:** These apps aren’t visible enough—I didn’t even know they existed.
- **Théo:** I’ve left many platforms because there were too many posts made just to criticize.
- **Julien:** On Letterboxd, there’s not really a proper recommendation feature.
Missing features:

- The classification is too broad (for example, in “horror” you see everything mixed together—subgenres aren’t specific enough).

- Subgenres aren’t highlighted.

- There should be an option to enable/disable recommendations on the homepage, based on watched movies.

- The recommendation algorithm isn’t good enough.

## Question 8 - Centralizing Content

**Is it difficult for you to centralize your movies, series, books, and games in one tool today?**
**If yes, why?**

- **Oscar:** No, because I don’t need to install multiple apps—everything’s in one place.
- **Valérie:** No, on the contrary—it’s convenient to have everything in one place without having to search endlessly.
- **Théo:** Not really, since it can also help discover new content. That’s why I use Google-you often stumble upon new stuff.
- **Julien:** Yes, because I struggle to neatly organize all four (games, films, series, books) without it becoming messy.

! Interesting insight: The transmedia aspect, offering cross-media recommendations (ex: a movie, a book, game, or series in the same universe or style).

## Question 9 - Secondary Features

**Which features would seem secondary or optional to you?**

- **Oscar:** Seeing time spent/media already consumed.
- **Valérie:** Don’t make features paywalled—I hate when things are locked behind a subscription.
- **Théo:** A “I’m feeling lucky” button like Google’s, that takes you to a random site or piece of content.
- **Julien:** On Letterboxd, I didn’t care at first about seeing others’ watchlists, but now I find it pretty cool.

**_User Needs_**

(What users consider necessary or essential)

## Question 10 - Essential Features

**What features seem essential to you in such an app?**

- **Oscar:** Recommendations.
- **Valérie:** Being able to chat with others, a real community aspect. I’d also like to add content not yet in the database, and list works I haven’t seen yet. Also, being able to add new media missing from the platform.
- **Théo:** Being able to share opinions and comment, with spoiler tags to protect other users.
- **Julien:** Being able to organize what I’ve seen/played, and track my progress (e.g. chapters or bookmarks). Also, having a summary of what I’ve seen, with unseen content blurred and unlockable by clicking (for shows/books).

## Question 11 - Recorded Information

**What info would you like to save for each piece of media? (rating, review, date, duration, status, platform, etc.)**

- **Oscar:** Time spent per media.
- **Valérie:** Add a rating, a review, and favorite moment from a film/series/book. Indicate who you watched it with (via groups). Add music that reminds you of it, and link related series/films/books in the same universe.
- **Théo:** Record what you’ve seen, with a simple “like / dislike” list.
- **Julien:** Add a rating, date watched, duration (or estimated duration), and an overall average rating.

## Question 12 - Personal Statistics

**Would you be interested in personal statistics? (time spent, number of works seen/read/played, average ratings, etc.)**

- **Oscar:** Yes, stats are always good to see.
- **Valérie:** Yes, that would be cool.
- **Théo:** Yes, I’m a fan of stats.
- **Julien:** Not too personalized—I’d feel like I’m being tracked.

## Question 13 - Account Privacy

**Would you prefer your account to be public or private? Why?**

- **Oscar:** Public by default, but with an option to go private.
- **Valérie:** The ability to choose what’s public or private depending on the content. For example, some books I’ve read I wouldn’t want everyone to know.
- **Théo:** Depends on what’s visible. Personal info shouldn’t appear. Only the username, account creation date, and liked posts should be visible—nothing more.
- **Julien:** Public username is fine—it’s a choice. But stats like time spent playing/reading shouldn’t be public.

## Question 14 - Simplicity of Use

**On a scale of 1 to 5, how important is simplicity of use?**
**(1 = not important, 5 = very important)**

- **Oscar:** 5
- **Valérie:** 5
- **Théo:** 5 — the more intuitive, the better.
- **Julien:** A strong 4.5

**_Expectations and Projections_**

(What users ideally want)

## Question 15 - Interest in a Centralized App

**Would you be interested in an app that centralizes movies, series, books, and games?**

- **Oscar:** Yes, recommendations are convenient.
- **Valérie:** Yes, that could be cool—it would help me discover new things based on what I like.
- **Théo:** I might try it once, but I’m not sure I’d use it long-term.
- **Julien:** I see why it’s a good idea. For me, books would add the most value.

## Question 16 - Recommendations

**Would you like to receive content recommendations based on your tastes and history ?**

- **Oscar:** Yes
- **Valérie:** Yes
- **Théo:** Yes
- **Julien:** Yes, but not AI-based—more on tags, similar actors, or directors.

## Question 17 - Notifications and Reminders

**Would you find it useful to receive reminders or notifications about new seasons, sequels, or episodes ?**

- **Oscar:** Yes, but with an option to disable them.
- **Valérie:** A big yes!
- **Théo:** Yes, that could be very interesting.
- **Julien:** Yes, if it’s content I actually follow.

## Question 18 - App Complexity

**Would you prefer a simple and quick app, or a more detailed one with many features ?**

- **Oscar:** Something in between — not too many menus, but still with plenty of features; it should stay simple to use.
- **Valérie:** Simple and fast—the simpler it is, the more I’ll use it.
- **Théo:** I don’t like when useful features are removed in the name of simplicity. I prefer a clean interface that stays full-featured.
- **Julien:** Simple and quick, with just a few options—not a thousand buttons everywhere.

## Question 19 - Interface Preferences

**Would you prefer a minimalist and clean interface, or a very visual one (posters, covers, images, etc.)?**

- **Oscar:** Minimalist.
- **Valérie:** Visual, but not overdone—it should be eye-catching without being cluttered.
- **Théo:** It’s better if it’s visually appealing, but accessibility comes first. I prefer something clean and minimal.
- **Julien:** A visually cool interface, but like Letterboxd—simple and elegant.

## Question 20 - Automatic Integrations

**Would you find it useful to automatically integrate with other services (Netflix, Steam, Kindle, etc.) to import your history or activity ?**

- **Oscar:** Optional, but rather interesting.
- **Valérie:** Yes.
- **Théo:** Yes, automating manual inputs would simplify things.
- **Julien:** No, I prefer doing it myself to control what’s visible or hidden.

## Question 21 - Ideal Feature

**If you could imagine an “ideal” or “magical” feature, what would it do ?**

- **Oscar:** /
- **Valérie:** Have a direct link to the movie, series, or game — hence the importance of integration with other platforms. Also, being able to contact the creator (e.g. the author of a book).
- **Théo:** Watch or read together in groups, like Discord watch parties or reading clubs.
- **Julien:** Add favorite cinemas (even if geolocation is intrusive). If friends want to see the same movie and share nearby theaters, send a notification like: “Go see this showing at this cinema and time!”

## Question 22 - Long-Term Adoption

**What would convince you to use this app long-term ?**

- **Oscar:** No harassment by notifications.
- **Valérie:** It shouldn’t be complicated, should include everything I want, be free, bug-free, and let me chat with my friends.
- **Théo:** I’m not a very loyal user, but a point and achievement system could be motivating—like medals for community participation or time spent, displayed on your profile (like Clash Royale).
- **Julien:** The ability to add books, have a community (social network aspect), and blend different media types.

## Question 23 - Uncool Things You Wouldn’t Want to See

- **Valérie:** Haters, and annoying “Subscribe now” notifications or popups.
- **Théo:** AI — too many sites already use auto-generated summaries that aren’t relevant.
- **Julien:** Not cool: rude private messages, and irrelevant AI-generated recommendations.
