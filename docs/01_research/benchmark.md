## Inspiration: Letterbox, Metacritic, Serializd, BookVillage

### Analysis of common features/functionality of Metacritic / Letterboxd / Serealizd / Book Village

- There is a rating system on all four sites, and ratings can be influenced by user comments.
- Promotion is based on ratings on some sites, such as Metacritic, while others, like Serealizd, do not display ratings directly; you have to click on the movie/series to see more details.

- On Serealizd and Letterboxd, ratings are also shown over time.  
  You can see that a movie or series was rated 2 stars a month ago and has risen to 4 stars,  
  while on Metacritic you can only see the current rating.

- Serealizd mainly uses images to better attract attention, unlike others that rely much more on text.

- Book Village displays a lot of information compared to others, but it does not have a rating system or an indication of CO2 per liter of water used for your order.

---

### Specific analysis of Serealizd

- The navigation method is vertical throughout the site.

- Serealizd uses a lot of images and very few buttons to make navigation very simple.

- Their statistics are organized by year; you can see the success of series over the course of the year.

- They use a simple tag system with an emoji in each tag to make category understanding easier.

- They have different sections under a selected series:  
  in Home you see seasons, in Reviews the comments, in Lists categories such as series available for streaming,  
  in Media posters, banners, and videos about the series, in Cast the actors, and in Similar Shows series of the same type.

- You can see on which platform the series is available for streaming.

- You can see when the next episode will be released, the last broadcast, and the first one.

- They also promote their social networks at the bottom of the page, as well as their app.

- In Discover, you can also see series by category.

- Serealizd has a pretty nice tag system that clearly explains the category of the movie/series.

### Specific analysis of Letterboxd

- Uses a very simple background to highlight the content in the center.

- At the top of the page, you can access the diary, and in Members you can see actors/directors and how many films they acted in.

- In Members, you can see a list of users with their favorite films, how many films they liked, and their playlists.

- In the middle of the page, you see a list of films; when hovering over them, you see the number of views and likes.

- A bit lower, you directly see user comments on films with their ratings.

- There is a message in the middle of the page explaining that you can keep track of every film you have ever watched,  
  create lists, and share opinions on films with a rating up to 5 stars and a comment,  
  compile and share reviews, and follow friends to read theirs.

- When you click on a film, you can see statistics about its rating, many comments, information about the studio, language,  
  the languages into which the film is translated, and the countries where it is available.  
  You also see the director and the whole production team.

- At the bottom of the page are contact information, help, and promotion of their social networks.

- At the bottom of the page, you also see ads on the left and at the very bottom.

### Specific analysis of Metacritic

- The page has bright colors that make series stand out.  
  You see in boxes a score out of 100 showing the popularity of the film, without seeing how it evolved over time.  
  The score is also characterized by colors from red to orange and displayed in a very large size.

- You can scroll horizontally through films/series to see if others interest you.

- A bit lower, you also see information about video games.

- When you click on a film/series, you see a presentation video and its score on the right.  
  If you scroll down, you see the list of all episodes; clicking on one shows its rating and comments.

- At the bottom of the page, once a series/film is selected, you see all comments with their score out of 100,  
  with the score very prominently displayed.

- At the very bottom of the page, you see information related to what you loaded and other info such as assistance,  
  digital services, privacy policy, and promotion of their social networks.

- Whether you click on a game, film, or series, it always works the same way: a score out of 100 with comments.

- At the top of the page, you can choose categories like games, films, music, TV series, and news,  
  with a large search button on the right and, next to it, a register button.

- Because the site is white, all colored buttons are very visible.

### Specific analysis of Book Village

- The site is white, which makes books and text stand out.

- On the page, you see several books categorized by condition, best sellers, and others,  
  and you also see the top 50 most appreciated authors.

- At the bottom of the page, you see articles, the help center, pricing, contacts, and promotion of their app.

- At the top of the page, you see a big image advertising their app.

- The page contains a lot of rather small text.

- At the top of the page, you see categories such as comics and manga, novels/literature, book series, school, science and technology, culture and religion, and practical arts.

- The account creation/login buttons are small on the right and not very highlighted,  
  unlike the “sell books” button next to them.

- There is a button at the top right of the page to change the theme to dark mode.
